Mathematical Modelling

This module explores mathematical modeling techniques for engineering and research systems, distinguishing between static and dynamic models, simulation validation, and optimization methods such as Genetic Algorithms and Particle Swarm Optimization.

Key Topics Explained

1. Concept of Mathematical Modelling: Modelling uses mathematics to represent real-world systems for analysis, prediction, and optimization.

2. Modeling of Engineering Systems: It simplifies complex engineering processes into mathematical forms using equations, variables, and constraints.

3. Static vs Dynamic Models: Static models represent systems at equilibrium (time-invariant), while dynamic models describe how systems evolve with time.

4. Model Prediction and Limitations: Models predict behavior under given inputs, but they are approximations limited by data accuracy and assumptions.

5. System Simulation: Simulation tests the model’s performance through computational tools before real-world implementation.

6. Model Validation: Validation checks if model outputs accurately reflect observed data; it ensures reliability of predictions.

7. Optimization Techniques: These improve model performance by finding optimal parameters that minimize or maximize a target function.

8. Genetic Algorithm (GA): A bio-inspired technique based on natural selection, mutation, and crossover to find global optima in complex systems.

9. Simulated Annealing (SA): Mimics the cooling process of metals to escape local minima and reach the global optimum solution.

10. Particle Swarm Optimization (PSO): A population-based optimization inspired by bird flocking behavior where particles update their positions toward the best-known solution.
