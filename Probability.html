Probability

This module introduces probability fundamentals, random variables, and common probability distributions including Binomial, Poisson, Normal, Exponential, and Uniform. It also covers measures of central tendency and dispersion essential for research data analysis.

Key Topics Explained

1. Conditional Probability: The probability of event A given that event B has occurred, written as P(A|B) = P(A∩B)/P(B).

2. Mean, Median, Mode: Mean is the average, Median is the middle value, and Mode is the most frequent observation — all describe central tendency.

3. Standard Deviation (SD): Measures how spread out data is from the mean; high SD means more variability.

4. Random Variables: A variable whose value is determined by chance; can be discrete (countable) or continuous (measurable range).

5. Uniform Distribution: All outcomes have equal probability. Example: rolling a fair die.

6. Normal Distribution: Symmetrical bell-shaped curve where mean = median = mode. Used widely in natural and social sciences.

7. Exponential Distribution: Describes time between independent events (e.g., time between arrivals at a queue).

8. Poisson Distribution: Models the number of events occurring in a fixed interval (e.g., number of calls received in an hour).

9. Binomial Distribution: Represents the number of successes in n independent Bernoulli trials with success probability p.

10. Applications in Research: These distributions and measures support data interpretation, uncertainty quantification, and inference in scientific analysis.
