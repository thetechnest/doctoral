Combinatorics

This module explores counting principles, permutations, combinations, recurrence relations, and generating functions. These form the mathematical foundation of algorithm design, discrete mathematics, and computational complexity analysis.

Key Topics Explained

1. Counting Principles: Fundamental rule of counting — if one event can occur in m ways and another in n ways, together they occur in m×n ways.

2. Permutations: Ordered arrangements of objects. The number of permutations of n distinct objects taken r at a time is P(n,r)=n!/(n−r)!.

3. Combinations: Unordered selections from a set. The number of combinations is C(n,r)=n!/[r!(n−r)!].

4. Summation: The process of adding a sequence of numbers; notations like Σ are used for compact expressions of sums.

5. Generating Functions: Used to represent sequences and solve recurrence relations. For a sequence a₀, a₁, a₂, … the generating function is G(x)=a₀+a₁x+a₂x²+….

6. Recurrence Relations: Express terms of a sequence in relation to previous terms (e.g., Fₙ = Fₙ₋₁ + Fₙ₋₂ for Fibonacci numbers).

7. Asymptotics: Describes the behavior of functions as inputs grow large — crucial in algorithmic complexity (e.g., O(n log n)).

8. Binomial Theorem: Expands powers of (a+b)ⁿ using combinations: (a+b)ⁿ=Σ C(n,k)aⁿ⁻ᵏbᵏ.

9. Inclusion–Exclusion Principle: Counts total elements in overlapping sets by adding and subtracting intersections.

10. Applications: Used in probability, cryptography, graph theory, and computational complexity for counting and estimation problems.
