Differential Privacy and Homomorphic Encryption

This module introduces two major privacy-preserving technologies ‚Äî Differential Privacy (DP) and Homomorphic Encryption (HE) ‚Äî that enable secure data sharing and computation in modern AI and research environments.

Key Topics Explained

1. Differential Privacy (DP): Adds random noise to data or results to prevent identification of individual records.

2. Œµ (Epsilon) Privacy Budget: Controls how much noise is added; smaller Œµ = stronger privacy, larger Œµ = higher accuracy.

3. Local vs. Global DP: Local DP adds noise before data leaves the device; Global DP adds noise at the aggregate level.

4. Mechanisms of DP: Laplace and Gaussian mechanisms are commonly used to inject controlled noise.

5. Homomorphic Encryption (HE): Enables computation on encrypted data without decryption, maintaining confidentiality.

6. Types of HE: Partial, Somewhat, and Fully Homomorphic Encryption ‚Äî differ by how many operations they support.

7. HE in AI: Allows federated learning systems to combine encrypted model updates securely.

8. Security Benefits: Both DP and HE prevent data leakage during AI training and sharing.

9. Limitations: DP may affect accuracy; HE can be computationally heavy ‚Äî trade-off between privacy and efficiency.

10. Combined Use (DP + HE): Offers dual protection: DP adds noise, and HE ensures encryption throughout computation.
