Academic Writing & Presentation

This module focuses on academic writing, proposal drafting, and effective presentation skills. It explores funding proposal structures, research communication, report writing, and oral defence techniques essential for doctoral success.

Key Topics Explained

1. Proposal Submission for Funding Agencies: Understand the format, guidelines, and expectations of agencies like DST, UGC, AICTE, and SERB when submitting proposals.

2. Elements of Style: Focus on clarity, brevity, and precision. Academic writing follows a formal tone and consistent style guide (APA, IEEE, or MLA).

3. Organization of Proposals: A good proposal includes sections like Abstract, Objectives, Methodology, Budget, and Expected Outcomes.

4. Funding Agencies Overview: Develop a basic understanding of national and international funding bodies that support research in diverse disciplines.

5. Research Report Writing: Reports must convey data, interpretation, and conclusions effectively with proper citation and formatting.

6. Communication Skills: Essential for presenting complex ideas clearly, both in written and oral form. Focus on audience-centric delivery.

7. Tailoring the Presentation: Adapt content and style based on the target audience — academic peers, evaluators, or industry professionals.

8. Oral Presentations & Posters: Effective visuals and clear speech help maintain engagement during seminars and conferences.

9. Submitting Research Articles: Follow publication ethics, select reputed journals, and ensure compliance with peer review guidelines.

10. Thesis & Report Writing: Thesis includes Introduction, Literature Review, Methodology, Results, and Discussion written in formal academic structure.

11. Elements of Excellent Presentation: Focus on Preparation, Visuals, and Delivery — the three pillars of impactful communication.

12. Oral Defence Skills: Prepare for Q&A sessions, demonstrate confidence, and respond with clarity supported by evidence.
