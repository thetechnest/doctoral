Research Resources and Ethics

This module introduces research resources, exploring sources of information, databases, and citation indices. It emphasizes ethical research practices including plagiarism prevention and intellectual property rights to ensure academic integrity.

Key Topics Explained

1. Sources of Information: Research information originates from primary sources (original data, experiments) and secondary sources (books, journals, reviews).

2. Literature Search: A structured approach to identify scholarly works using academic databases, journals, and references aligned with the research goal.

3. World Wide Web & Online Databases: Databases such as IEEE Xplore, ScienceDirect, and Scopus enable global access to research materials.

4. Citation Indices & Impact Factor: Citation indices track references; impact factor assesses the average citations per journal, indicating influence.

5. Literature Review, Case Studies & Meta Analysis: A literature review consolidates previous findings; meta analysis merges quantitative data for stronger insights.

6. Role of Librarian: Librarians play a key role in information retrieval, database access, and supporting citation ethics in academic research.

7. Ethical and Moral Issues in Research: Includes honesty, objectivity, integrity, and avoiding data fabrication or falsification.

8. Plagiarism and Tools: Plagiarism is uncredited reuse of others’ work. Tools like Turnitin and Grammarly help maintain originality.

9. Intellectual Property Rights: Protects intellectual outputs through patents, copyrights, and trademarks.

10. Patent & Copyright Laws: Patents protect inventions; copyright protects creative and literary works from unauthorized use.
